About the Author

Joanne Rowling known by her pen name J. K. Rowling, is a British author and philanthropist. She wrote Harry Potter, a seven-volume fantasy series published from 1997 to 2007. The series has sold over 600 million copies, been translated into 84 languages, and spawned a global media franchise including films and video games. The Casual Vacancy (2012) was her first novel for adults. She writes Cormoran Strike, an ongoing crime fiction series, under the alias Robert Galbraith.

2. Harry Potter & Prisoner of Azkaban

Prisoner of Azkaban is a turning point in the series, offering a darker and more complex story than its predecessors. The escaped convict Sirius Black casts a shadow over Hogwarts, while Dementors, soul-sucking creatures guarding the school, create a tense atmosphere. This book delves deeper into Harry's past and the mystery surrounding his parents' deaths. It's not all gloom, however, as Professor Lupin brings a warm presence and the time-turner adds a fascinating twist. While the darker tone might surprise some readers, the fast-paced plot, rich character development, and thrilling mystery solidify Prisoner of Azkaban as a favorite among fans.
